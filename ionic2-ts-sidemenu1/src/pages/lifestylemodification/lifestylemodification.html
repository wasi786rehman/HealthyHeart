Lifestyle Modification

 

  1. HEART HEALTHY DIET

A healthy diet is one of the best weapons you have to fight cardiovascular disease. The food you eat (and the amount) can affect other controllable risk factors: cholesterol, blood pressure, diabetes and overweight.

 

TIPS:

  • Eat a diet rich in vegetables .You should aim to 400-500g or five servings (5 servings x 80g) of fruits and vegetables per day.
  • Eat a variety of seasonal fruits and vegetables (both cooked and as a salad).
  • Include whole legumes such as moong daal, masoor daal,1 handful of nuts like walnut, almond, and seeds like pumpkin seed, melon seeds etc.
  • High-fibre foods such as beans, sprouts, and whole grains are a great choice. Choose whole grain bread, cereal, and rice.
  • Choose low-fat dairy products such as toned milk, skimmed milk, home-made paneer, chach, lassi etc.
  • Eat fish at least twice a week.
  • Select skinless chicken and choose lean cuts of meat. But, limit your intake of red meats such as mutton, beef.
  • Limit your oil consumption to 3-4 tsp of oil per day.
  • Practice alternating/blending of your cooking oil. Use different type of oils such as refined oil, sunflower oil,soyabean oil , olive oil.
  • Avoid dalda, ghee, vanaspati etc.
  • Limit how much saturated fat, trans fat and added sugars you eat. Such as baked goods, biscuits, cakes etc.
  • Limit your sugar consumption to 3-4 tsp per day if you are not having diabetes.
  • If you are diabetic take no or less sugar. Reduce consumption of simple sugars that are found in soda, candy and sugary desserts.
  • Choose and prepare foods with little or no salt (sodium). Aim to consume no more than 1,500 mg of sodium per day.
  • Cut down on salt. Use 3⁄4 tsp of salt per day. Do not sprinkle extra salt on your food.
  • Use seasonings instead of salt to enhance the taste of food like lemon juice,vinegar,basil,garlic etc.
  • Avoid use of papad, pickles, packaged food etc.
  • Avoid consuming fast foods like burgers, fries, samosa, kachori, paranthas, sweets etc. It is one of the easiest way to reduce your intake of saturated fats. 
  • Eating a small amount of unsaturated fat will increase the level of good cholesterol. Examples are oily fish, nuts and seeds, sunflower,olive oil etc.
  • Avoid consuming white rice .Substitute it with brown rice.
  1. BE PHYSICALLY ACTIVE:

Resume your physical activity gradually after surgery. Be physically active every day. Your doctor and physiotherapist will develop an exercise program that is safe and beneficial. They will consider your fitness level, heart health, any physical limitations, the amount, intensity and duration of exercise needed to improve heart health, and the need for supervision. Being physically active is important to prevent heart disease.

 

Important points:

Following points you should keep in your mind while doing exercises:

  • Frequency – The recommended frequency of exercise is 3-5times a week.
  • Content and duration – It is important that each session consist of :
  • Warm-up phase -5- to 10-minute,
  • Conditioning phase -at least 20 minutes,
  • Cool-down phase –5- to 10-minute
  • Intensity –Intensity of exercise should be moderate.
  • Exercise progression – Over time, most people can gradually increase the level of exercise in the workout. Beneficial exercise can also be built in to the daily routine by taking a brisk walk or enjoying active play with children or grandchildren.

Type of Exercise

Following exercises are recommended after the surgery:

  • Aerobics: walking, jogging, cycling, stairs climbing.
  • Flexibility training: Upper and lower body ROM
  • Coughing exercises: Huff coughing
  • Breathing exercises: pursed lip breathing, diaphragmatic breathing, incentive spirometry.
  • Relaxation exercises: warm up and cool down, deep breathing.
  • Strengthening exercises: Bridging, single leg raises, quadriceps.

 

 

 

PRECAUTIONS:

 

  • If you notice that you are having difficulty in doing your exercise program than usual,then slow down or STOP if necessary.

 

Signs that you should stop your activity

  • Pain or discomfort in the chest, neck, jaw, arms, back etc
  • Excessive sweating
  • Nausea
  • Irregular heart beat
  • Extreme shortness of breath
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Dizziness etc

 

  • If these symptoms settle after 2 to 5 minutes of rest, rest for a further 5 to 10 minutes & then continue exercising at a slower rate.
  • If the symptoms are not relieved after the initial 2 to 5 minute of rest take nitroglycerine (if prescribed)

as directed.

  • If symptoms have not settled within 15 to 20 minutes contact your doctor.
  1. MANAGE YOUR WEIGHT: Managing your weight can help you to prevent heart diseases .Heart healthy diet, controlling calorie intake and physical activity are the some of the key factors to maintain a healthy weight.

BMI: Your Body Mass Index (BMI) can help tell you if your weight is healthy. Reach and maintain a healthy weight (body mass index less than 25 kg/m2).

Waist circumference: Ideal waist circumference of males should be < 94 cm (males); and for females

< 80 cm.

  1. STOP SMOKING /TOBACCO USE

Stop smoking or using tobacco. It will reduce your risk of premature death, heart diseases, diabetes, and obesity.

 

TIPS:

  • Decide on a Quit Day and ask your family and friends to support you.
  • Ask your healthcare provider for information, programs and medications that may help.
  • Go where smoking isn’t allowed, and avoid being around people who smoke.
  • Keep busy doing things that make it hard to smoke.
  • Nicotine gums, lozenges sprays can be used.

 

  1. LIMIT YOUR ALCOHOL

 

Drinking too much alcohol can raise blood pressure, increase cardiomyopathy , stroke, cancer, and other diseases. However, there is a cardio protective effect of moderate alcohol consumption. If you drink, limit your alcohol consumption to no more than two drinks per day for men and no more than one drink per day for women. It's not recommended that non drinkers start using alcohol or that drinker’s increase the amount they drink.

  • One drink is defined as Beer- 355mL
  • Wine-148mL
  • 80-proof distilled spirits-44mL
  1. REDUCE STRESS

 

There is a relationship between coronary heart disease risk and stress in a person's life that may affect the risk factors for heart disease and stroke. For example, people under stress may overeat, start smoking or smoke more than they otherwise would. Depression, hostility, anger, and low social support are associated with heart disease, after controlling for adverse health behaviors. These risk factors are unfortunately increased in lower socio-economic groups

TIPS:

  • Practice healthy techniques for managing stress such as relaxation exercises, yoga, meditation
  • Chat with family and friends.
  • Get enough sleep, 6-8 hours per day.
  • Pursue a hobby like reading, writing, music, and painting.

  1. RESUMING SEXUAL ACTIVITY

 

An important issue for many patients who have had bypass surgery is when sexual activity can be safely resumed. In the first few weeks after an uncomplicated heart attack, most people are at high risk of heart-related problems during sex as a result of a rise in the heart rate and blood pressure. However, this risk becomes much smaller by 6-8weeks after surgery.

 

TIPS:

  • Assume positions which can avoid strain on sternum and incision.
  • Extra pillows can help you to feel comfortable.
  1. FOLLOW UP:

 

 Follow-up care is of great importance since people who have had bypass surgery have a significantly increased risk of more cardiac events, including recurrent chest pain, heart attack, heart failure, and an increased risk of dying. The risk of these problems is greatly reduced by closely following a clinician's recommendations for rehabilitation, follow-up visits, and treatments. Over time, the treatment plan may change as heart health improves or other medical problems develop.

 

TIPS:

 

  • Follow up your doctor on time.
  • Get your investigations done on time.
  • Check your B.P, blood sugar level as advised and record it.
  • Maintain diary, set reminders and involve family and friends.

Walking: walking is an excellent exercise for your heart. Walk for 45-60 minutes per day at a slow to moderate walking speed.

Jogging: start with short periods of jogging interspersed with equal distance of walking. As you progress walk less and jog more.

Cycling: You can use stationary cycles at home. Start with a period of 10 minutes with easy pedalling against light or no resistance than gradually increase the duration and intensity.

Stairs climbing: Initially ascend gradually up to 16 steps and descend slowly. Stop and take rest in between steps.

Huff –coughing: Practice 2 sets of 5 repetitions.

ROM: Perform range of motion exercises 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions’ on shoulder, elbow, hand and wrist, hip and knee ankle and foot.

Pursed lip breathing: Practice pursed lip breathing 3 sets of 5 repetitions and whenever you remember.

Diaphragmatic breathing: Practice 3 sets of 5 repetitions daily.

Incentive spirometry: Practice 3 sets of 10 repetitions.

Deep breathing: Practice 5 sets of 3 repetitions and in between activities.

Bridging: Practice 5sets of 10 repetitions.

Single leg raises: Practice5 sets of 10 repetitions.

Quadriceps :Practice 5 set of 10 repetitions.
